Step-by-Step

International Enrollment Process

Follow these steps in order. Each step must be completed before moving to the next. PTC will guide you through the whole process.

01

Contact PTC & Submit Your Application

Email or message PTC with your personal details, nationality, and intended program. We'll review your application and issue an official Acceptance Letter — the document that starts everything else.

✓ No cost
02

Receive Your Form I-20 from PTC

Once accepted, PTC issues your I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status). This certifies you as an enrolled student in our Part 141 program. You must provide proof of sufficient funds to cover your full training cost to receive your I-20.

I-20 Processing: ~$150–$350
📄 Proof of funds required: Bank statement dated within 30 days showing enough to cover full training ($65,000 career package minimum) plus living expenses.
03

Complete TSA / FTSP Security Clearance

All non-US citizens must complete the Flight Training Security Program (FTSP) through the TSA at flightschoolcandidates.gov. Create an account, submit your application listing PTC as your training provider, pay the vetting fee, and complete fingerprinting.

TSA Fee: ~$130 Fingerprinting: ~$100
⏱ Timeline: 3 days (US/Canadian citizens), up to 30 days for other nationalities. Apply early.
04

Apply for Your M-1 Student Visa

With your I-20 and TSA clearance confirmed, apply for an M-1 Vocational Student Visa at your nearest US Embassy or Consulate. Complete the DS-160 form online, pay the SEVIS and visa fees, and schedule your embassy interview.

SEVIS I-901 Fee: $350 DS-160 Visa Fee: $185
📋 Bring to your interview: Valid passport, I-20, DS-160 confirmation, SEVIS receipt, TSA clearance, proof of funds, acceptance letter, evidence of ties to home country.
05

Book Travel to Southern California

Once your M-1 visa is stamped, book your flight to Ontario International Airport (ONT) — just 20 minutes from Brackett Field. You may enter the US up to 30 days before your program start date. Notify PTC of your arrival date so we can prepare your enrollment.

✓ No additional government fees
06

Arrive, Get Your FAA Medical & Start Training

On arrival, report to PTC and complete your formal enrollment. You'll then visit a local FAA Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) for your 3rd Class Medical Certificate, get your free Student Pilot Certificate through IACRA online, set your schedule with your CFI, and begin your Part 141 curriculum.

FAA Medical: ~$75–$150 Student Pilot Cert: Free

💰 Total Budget to Arrive Ready

Plan to arrive with enough to cover: $65,000 training + ~$1,200 pre-training fees + 3–6 months living expenses (~$6,000–$12,000). Total recommended: $75,000–$80,000 USD.

🏠 Living in La Verne, CA

Budget $1,200–$2,000/month for housing (shared apartments available near Brackett Airport). Monthly total including food, transport, and personal: ~$1,500–$2,500. La Verne is safe, quiet, and close to everything you need.

✈️ Nearest Airport: ONT

Ontario International Airport (ONT) is just 20 minutes from Brackett Field and is far less stressful than LAX. Most international connections are available via ONT, LAX, or SNA (John Wayne Airport, ~45 min).

⚖️ We Are Not Immigration Attorneys

PTC provides enrollment support and required school documentation. For personal immigration questions, always consult a qualified US immigration attorney. Visa rules vary by nationality and can change.
