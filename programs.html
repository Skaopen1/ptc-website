Program Comparison

Part 141 vs. Part 61

Both paths lead to the same FAA certificates. The difference is structure and pacing.

Part 141

Structured Career Training

FAA-approved syllabus with stage checks, lesson plans, and structured progress reviews.

  • FAA-approved structured training course outline (TCO)
  • Required stage checks at defined milestones
  • Reduced minimum flight hours (35 hrs for PPL vs. 40)
  • Accepted for international student M-1 visas
  • TSA Alien Flight Student Program (AFSP) eligible
  • Ideal for full-time, dedicated students
  • Consistent, audited instruction quality
  • I-20 issuance support for qualifying international students
Enroll in Part 141
Part 61

Flexible & Personalized

Train on your schedule with no fixed syllabus. Instruction is tailored to your pace and goals.

  • No strict syllabus — train at your own pace
  • No stage check requirements
  • Standard minimums: 40 hrs total for PPL
  • Perfect for working professionals and weekend flyers
  • Customize your training path with your CFI
  • No enrollment deadlines or course timelines
  • Great for recreational pilots and career changers
  • Available to US citizens and permanent residents
Enroll in Part 61
Your Pathway

From Zero to Commercial Pilot

PTC offers the full progression. Here's where your journey takes you.

01

Private Pilot License (PPL)

Your first certificate. Fly solo and carry passengers under visual flight rules (VFR). Ground school, written exam, and checkride included.

Part 141: ~35 hrs Part 61: ~40 hrs Est. Duration: 3–5 months
02

Instrument Rating (IR)

Fly in clouds and low visibility using cockpit instruments only. Required for most professional aviation careers. Builds serious pilot judgment.

Part 141: ~35 hrs instrument Part 61: ~50 hrs cross-country Est. Duration: 3–4 months
03

Commercial Pilot License (CPL)

The professional step. Allows you to be compensated for flying. Prerequisite for airline careers, charter, banner towing, and flight instruction.

Part 141: 190 total hrs Part 61: 250 total hrs Est. Duration: 4–6 months
