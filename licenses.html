PPL · First Certificate

Private Pilot License

The gateway to aviation. Your PPL allows you to fly single-engine aircraft solo and carry passengers under visual flight rules (VFR). This is where every pilot's journey begins — and PTC will make yours unforgettable.

Min. Flight Hours
35–40 hrs
Est. Duration
3–5 months
Min. Age
17 years
Medical Required
3rd Class
  • Fly solo — you're in command
  • Carry passengers on VFR flights
  • Prerequisite for all advanced ratings
  • Ground school + written exam + checkride
  • Cross-country and night flying included
Start PPL Training
02
IFR · Instrument Rating

Instrument Rating

Fly in clouds, low visibility, and real IFR conditions using only cockpit instruments. The Instrument Rating is the most critical step toward a professional aviation career and dramatically expands where and when you can fly.

Min. Flight Hours
50 hrs XC (P61)
Est. Duration
3–4 months
Prerequisite
PPL
Medical Required
3rd Class
  • Fly in IMC — clouds, fog, low visibility
  • Navigate airways using cockpit instruments
  • Required for commercial airline career
  • Approach procedures, holds, SIDs/STARs
  • Dramatically improves flight safety skills
Start IFR Training
03
CPL · Get Paid to Fly

Commercial Pilot License

The license that turns your passion into a profession. A CPL allows you to be compensated for flying — opening doors to airline careers, charter, cargo, banner towing, aerial photography, and more. This is the professional milestone.

Min. Total Hours
190–250 hrs
Est. Duration
4–6 months
Prerequisite
PPL + IFR
Medical Required
2nd Class
  • Get paid to fly — legally
  • Higher aircraft performance standards
  • Required for airline first officer position
  • Advanced maneuvers and precision flying
  • Prerequisite for CFI certificate
Start CPL Training
04
MEL · Multi-Engine Rating

Multi-Engine Rating

Expand your capabilities to twin-engine aircraft. The Multi-Engine Rating is essential for airline careers, corporate aviation, and any operation requiring more than one engine. Learn engine-out procedures, asymmetric thrust, and advanced systems.

No Min. Hours
Proficiency-based
Est. Duration
2–4 weeks
Prerequisite
PPL or CPL
Added As
Rating Add-on
  • Fly twin-engine aircraft
  • Engine-out emergency procedures
  • Greatly expands hiring eligibility
  • Required for most airline positions
  • Can be added to PPL or CPL certificate
Start Multi-Engine Training
05
CFI · Certified Flight Instructor

Flight Instructor (CFI)

Teach others to fly while building the 1,500 hours needed for airline hiring. The CFI certificate is the most common path to building flight time — and the best way to cement your own skills. Many CFIs instruct right here at PTC.

Min. Total Hours
250 hrs total
Est. Duration
2–3 months
Prerequisite
CPL
Medical Required
2nd Class
  • Get paid to build flight hours
  • Teach PPL, solo, and cross-country training
  • Master aviation knowledge at the deepest level
  • Fastest route to 1,500-hour ATP minimum
  • Opportunity to instruct at PTC
Start CFI Training
06
CFII · Instrument Instructor

Flight Instructor — Instrument

Teach instrument flying. The CFII allows you to instruct students pursuing their Instrument Rating, which is one of the most in-demand CFI positions at flight schools. Higher earning potential and a broader student base.

Prerequisite
CFI + Instrument Rating
Est. Duration
4–8 weeks
Added As
Rating Add-on
Exam
FOI + IGI + Checkride
  • Teach students their Instrument Rating
  • Higher instructor earning potential
  • Deepen your own instrument proficiency
  • Highly sought after at flight schools
  • Pairs perfectly with CFI certificate
Start CFII Training
