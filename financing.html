Common Questions

Financing FAQ

Do I need good credit to get a flight training loan?

Most aviation lenders do perform a credit check. AOPA Finance and Pilot Finance are designed for aspiring pilots and are generally more flexible than traditional banks. Having a co-signer can improve your odds and your interest rate if your credit isn't perfect.

Can I finance just part of my training?

Yes. There are no minimums with most aviation lenders. You can finance just your PPL, or take a line of credit and draw only what you need as you progress. AOPA's revolving credit line is ideal for this — borrow what you need, when you need it.

Are international students eligible for financing?

Most US-based aviation lenders require US citizenship or permanent residency. International students on M-1 visas are generally not eligible for US loans. International students should plan to arrive with personal funding or explore financing options in their home country before coming to the US.

What's the typical monthly payment on a $65,000 loan?

At a 7–10% interest rate over 10 years, a $65,000 loan would cost approximately $450–$550/month. Once you're flying commercially as a first officer earning $80K+/year, that payment is very manageable. Think of it as an investment — not a debt.

Can I combine a loan with pay-as-you-go?

Absolutely. Many students use a loan to cover the bulk of training (like the $65K career package) and pay smaller incidentals — books, test fees, additional hours — out of pocket. Talk to PTC and your lender to structure the best plan for your situation.
