Why Brackett Field Is One of the Best Places to Learn to Fly in California

November 30, 2024 · By Kareem M., Chief Flight Instructor · 6 min read
← Back to Blog

When people picture learning to fly in California, they often think of Santa Monica, Van Nuys, or one of the big airports in the LA basin. But if you ask experienced flight instructors where they'd choose to train a student from scratch, Brackett Field — ICAO identifier KPOC, located in La Verne in the San Gabriel Valley — comes up time and time again. Here's why we think Brackett Field gives our students a genuinely exceptional training environment.

The Weather: 300+ VFR Days Per Year

For a flight school, nothing matters more than weather. Cancelled lessons break training momentum, increase cost, and push completion dates further out. La Verne sits in the eastern San Gabriel Valley, just far enough inland from the LA basin to escape the coastal marine layer that regularly socks in airports like Santa Monica (KSMO) and Hawthorne (KHHR).

The result: we average well over 300 VFR flying days per year. When a student books a lesson at PTC, the lesson happens. That consistency allows students to fly three to five days per week without the weather cancellations that plague coastal California schools, and it translates directly into faster completion times and lower total training costs.

The Airspace: Complex, But Manageable

Brackett Field sits in one of the most interesting airspace environments in the country. Here's what's nearby:

  • Ontario International Airport (KONT) — a Class C airport just 5 miles to the south, with approach and departure control that students regularly talk to on cross-country flights
  • Los Angeles Class B airspace — within student cross-country range, providing exposure to busy terminal area operations
  • San Gabriel Mountains — terrain that demands proper mountain flying awareness and builds real-world pilot skills
  • Multiple nearby training airports — Cable Airport (KCCB), Chino Airport (KCNO), and Fullerton (KFUL) provide excellent cross-country destinations and additional practice environments

Why does this matter? Because training in complex airspace with real ATC, real Class C and Class B encounters, and real terrain builds pilots who are actually ready for real-world flying. A student who has only ever flown over flat farmland in uncontrolled airspace will struggle in their first job dealing with busy terminal areas. Our students arrive at their first checkride — and their first job — with real experience under their belts.

Cross-country options from Brackett: Within a 2-hour flight, our students can reach the coast at Long Beach, the desert at Palm Springs, the mountains at Big Bear, and the valleys at Riverside and San Bernardino — each with completely different terrain, weather patterns, and airspace. That variety is enormously valuable for developing well-rounded pilot skills.

The Airport Itself: Student-Friendly and Well-Run

Brackett Field is a Los Angeles County-owned and operated airport. It has two parallel runways (26L/8R and 26R/8L), excellent lighting for night operations, an ATIS, a staffed control tower during operational hours, and a friendly, professional environment. The tower controllers know that Brackett is a busy training airport and are generally patient and communicative with student pilots — something that genuinely matters when you're still building your radio confidence.

The airport hosts several businesses, a restaurant, and an active general aviation community. On any given weekend, the ramp is alive with activity — which creates a motivating atmosphere for students who are working toward their goals.

The Mountain Backdrop

We can't talk about Brackett without mentioning the scenery. The San Gabriel Mountains rise to over 10,000 feet just a few miles north of the airport. Mount Baldy, the Cucamonga Wilderness, and the ridge lines of the Angeles National Forest are literally in your windshield from the moment you climb out on the 26 departure. On a clear morning after a winter storm has dusted the peaks with snow, there are few more spectacular places to be flying a small airplane in all of Southern California.

Beyond aesthetics, those mountains create the terrain awareness training environment that every serious pilot needs. Mountain wave, density altitude, and terrain avoidance aren't abstract concepts at Brackett — they're part of the real world you navigate every time you fly.

Come See It For Yourself

The best way to appreciate Brackett Field as a training environment is to fly from it. Book a discovery flight with PTC and we'll take you up over the valley, point out the local landmarks, and let you take the controls. Get in touch and we'll set it up. We're proud of where we fly, and we love sharing it.
