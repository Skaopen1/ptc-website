Admissions

Starting your aviation journey is simpler than you think. Here's exactly how to get started at PTC — whether you're local or coming from abroad.

For US Students

Domestic Enrollment Process

Five simple steps from inquiry to first flight.

01

Reach Out & Schedule a Tour

Contact PTC via phone, email, or our contact form. We'll invite you to Brackett Airport to meet the team, see the aircraft, and tour the facility. No commitment required.

02

Book a Discovery Flight

Before enrolling, take a discovery flight with a certified instructor. You'll take the controls over La Verne and get a feel for what training will be like. It's the best first step.

03

Choose Your Program

Decide between Part 141 (structured, career-track) or Part 61 (flexible, personalized). Your instructor will help you pick the right fit based on your goals and availability.

04

Obtain Your FAA Medical Certificate

All student pilots need at least a 3rd Class FAA Medical Certificate from an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). We can help connect you with one nearby.

05

Enroll & Begin Training

Complete enrollment paperwork, get your student pilot certificate, set your schedule with your CFI, and start flying. Your aviation career begins here.

For International Students

International Admissions

PTC's Part 141 program is TSA AFSP approved. We support international students every step of the way.

01

Contact PTC & Get Acceptance Letter

Reach out to us to confirm your interest and eligibility. We'll issue an official acceptance letter required for your TSA AFSP application.

02

Apply via TSA AFSP Portal

Submit your TSA Alien Flight Student Program application at flightschoolcandidates.gov. Pay the required TSA vetting fee and complete background check.

03

Receive TSA Approval

Wait for TSA clearance (typically 2–4 weeks). PTC will receive confirmation when you're cleared to begin training.

04

Obtain US Visa (if applicable)

For non-citizens needing a visa, PTC can provide the documentation required for an M-1 student visa application through your local US embassy or consulate.

05

Arrive & Begin Training

Arrive at Brackett Airport, complete your FAA medical, enroll formally, and begin your Part 141 curriculum. Welcome to California and welcome to PTC!

📋 TSA AFSP Requirements

The following documents and steps are required by the TSA for all non-US citizens seeking flight training in the United States:

  • Valid passport from your country of citizenship
  • Acceptance letter from PTC (we provide this)
  • Completed TSA AFSP online application
  • TSA vetting fee payment (amount varies by training type)
  • Biometric fingerprinting at a designated location
  • Background check clearance from TSA (2–4 weeks)
  • US visa or entry authorization (if required for your nationality)
  • FAA Medical Certificate from a certified AME in the US

🌍 Countries We've Welcomed

We have helped students from Saudi Arabia, UAE, Jordan, Morocco, Egypt, Mexico, Brazil, Philippines, South Korea, and many more countries earn their FAA certificates at PTC.

Prerequisites

What You Need to Get Started

🎂

Age Requirements

Solo flight: minimum 16 years old. PPL certificate: minimum 17. Student pilots of any eligible age are welcome to begin ground training.

🏥

Medical Certificate

A 3rd Class FAA Medical Certificate is required. Issued by an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). We can connect you with one near La Verne.

🗣️

English Proficiency

FAA requires all pilots to be able to read, speak, write, and understand English. Radio communication is conducted in English.

📚

No Prior Experience

Zero flight hours? Perfect. We start from the very beginning. All you need is the passion to fly — we'll handle the rest.

Questions About Enrollment?

We're happy to walk you through the process personally. Reach out and we'll respond within one business day.
