✈️ Getting Started

None whatsoever. PTC accepts students with zero flight experience. Your very first lesson starts from scratch — preflight inspection, taxiing, takeoff, and basic flight maneuvers. Your instructor is with you every step of the way.

A discovery flight is a short introductory flight where you take the controls of a Cessna 172N with a certified flight instructor beside you. PTC offers two options: a Hollywood Sign tour for $220 and a Catalina Island tour for $300. It's the best way to find out if flying is right for you before committing to a program.

You can begin ground school at any age. The FAA requires you to be at least 16 years old to fly solo and at least 17 to earn your Private Pilot License. There is no upper age limit — pilots of all ages train and fly successfully.

For a Private Pilot License you need a 3rd Class FAA Medical Certificate, issued by an FAA Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). For a Commercial License you'll need a 2nd Class. PTC can connect you with an AME near La Verne. Most healthy people have no issues passing the exam.

📋 Programs & Training

Part 141 uses an FAA-approved structured syllabus with stage checks at defined milestones, and allows reduced minimum flight hours. It's best for full-time, career-track students and is required for international students on M-1 visas. Part 61 is more flexible with no fixed syllabus — ideal for part-time students, working adults, or anyone who needs to train around a busy schedule. Both lead to the same FAA certificates.

Most students complete their PPL in 3–5 months when training 2–3 days per week. Full-time students training 5 days a week have completed it in as little as 6–8 weeks. The FAA minimum is 35 hours (Part 141) or 40 hours (Part 61), though the national average is around 60–70 hours.

All primary training at PTC is conducted in our fleet of six Cessna 172N Skyhawks — the world's most-produced training aircraft. The 172N is powered by a 150HP Lycoming O-320 engine with classic steam gauges, building foundational piloting skills that transfer to any aircraft.

PTC offers training for six FAA certificates and ratings: Private Pilot License (PPL), Instrument Rating (IFR), Commercial Pilot License (CPL), Multi-Engine Rating, Flight Instructor Certificate (CFI), and Instrument Flight Instructor Certificate (CFII). See our Licenses page for full details.

💰 Cost & Financing

Based on our hourly rates ($240/hr dual, $170/hr solo), a PPL typically costs $15,000–$22,000 depending on the hours needed. PTC also offers a $65,000 all-inclusive career package covering PPL + Instrument Rating + Commercial Pilot License. See our Tuition page for full details.

PTC operates on a pay-as-you-go basis with no large upfront fees. For financing, we recommend AOPA Finance (up to $100,000, $0 down) and Flight Training Finance (pilotfinance.com). See our Financing page for lender options and details. Note: financing is generally only available to US citizens and permanent residents.

The $65,000 Zero-to-Commercial package is all-inclusive: all aircraft rental, all CFI instruction hours, ground school, study materials, all three written exam fees, and all three checkride fees for PPL, Instrument Rating, and Commercial Pilot License. No hidden add-ons. Starting point: zero flight experience required.

🌍 International Students

Absolutely. PTC's Part 141 program is TSA/FTSP approved and we issue I-20 forms for students pursuing M-1 student visas. We have trained students from over 17 countries including Saudi Arabia, UAE, Jordan, Morocco, Egypt, and many more. See our International Admissions page.

Most international flight students need an M-1 Vocational Student Visa. You'll need an I-20 from PTC, TSA/FTSP clearance (~$230), and a US Embassy interview. The full process typically takes 2–4 months. See our Visa & TSA Guide for step-by-step details and cost breakdown.

FAA certificates are internationally recognized under ICAO. Many countries allow direct conversion of FAA licenses to their national equivalent. Requirements vary — check with your home country's civil aviation authority (CAA) for the specific conversion process.

📍 Brackett Airport & Location

PTC is located at Brackett Field Airport (FAA identifier: POC) in La Verne, California — approximately 30 miles east of downtown Los Angeles in the San Gabriel Valley. The closest international airport is Ontario International (ONT), about 20 minutes away.

Southern California offers some of the best flying weather in the United States. La Verne and the San Gabriel Valley see over 300 VFR days per year. The region rarely experiences the weather delays common in other parts of the country, making it ideal for efficient, uninterrupted training.
