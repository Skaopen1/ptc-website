Your Roadmap

Step-by-Step Process

From your home country to the cockpit at Brackett Airport — here's every step, in order.

01

Contact PTC & Get Accepted

Reach out via our contact form or email. We'll review your application and issue an official Acceptance Letter — required for your TSA and visa applications.

✓ No cost at this stage
02

Receive Your I-20 Form

PTC will issue your Form I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status). This is the document your US Embassy needs to issue your M-1 visa. You must be enrolled in our Part 141 program to receive an I-20.

Fee: ~$150–$350 (I-20 processing)
📌 Important: You must demonstrate proof of sufficient funds to cover full training costs when requesting your I-20.
03

Apply for TSA / FTSP Clearance

All non-US citizens must complete a security threat assessment through the TSA's Flight Training Security Program (FTSP) — formerly AFSP. Apply at flightschoolcandidates.gov. PTC must be listed as your training provider.

TSA Fee: ~$130 USD Fingerprinting: ~$100 USD
⏱ Processing time: Typically 3–30 days. US citizens and Canadian citizens may be processed in as little as 3 days. Others may take up to 30 days.
04

Apply for Your M-1 Student Visa

With your I-20 and TSA clearance in hand, apply for an M-1 Vocational Student Visa at your nearest US Embassy or Consulate. Complete the DS-160 form online, pay the visa application fee, and attend your embassy interview.

Visa fee: $185 USD (DS-160) SEVIS fee: $350 USD
📋 Bring to your interview: Valid passport, I-20, DS-160 confirmation, SEVIS receipt, proof of funds, acceptance letter, and ties to your home country.
05

Book Your Flight to Los Angeles

Once your M-1 visa is approved, you're cleared to travel! Book your flight to LAX, ONT (Ontario), or SNA — all within easy reach of Brackett Airport in La Verne, CA. You may enter the US up to 30 days before your program start date.

✓ No additional government fees
06

Arrive, Settle, & Start Training

PTC will help you get oriented — find housing near Brackett Airport, obtain your FAA Medical Certificate from a local Aviation Medical Examiner (AME), get your student pilot certificate, and begin your Part 141 training curriculum.

FAA Medical: ~$75–$150 Student pilot cert: Free (online)

💰 Proof of Funds Required

When applying for your I-20, you must show a bank statement (dated within 30 days) demonstrating sufficient funds to cover your full training cost — at minimum, the $65,000 career package or your chosen program cost, plus living expenses.

🏠 Living in La Verne, CA

La Verne is a safe, quiet city in the San Gabriel Valley east of Los Angeles. Budget approximately $1,200–$2,000/month for housing (shared apartments are available), plus food, transportation, and personal expenses. Your total monthly living cost is typically $1,500–$2,500.

🛂 Visa Duration & Extensions

An M-1 visa is issued for the duration of your program plus 30 days. If your training takes longer than expected, you can apply for an extension through USCIS. PTC will help you with documentation for any required extensions. Do not overstay your visa status.

📱 What to Do On Arrival

Upon arriving in the US: report to PTC within your program start window, apply for a Social Security Number (optional but useful), open a US bank account, obtain a California State ID or driver's license, and get your FAA Medical Certificate before your first flight.

Visa Guide

Which Visa Do You Need?

Most international flight students need one of two visa types — here's the difference.

Recommended for Most Students

M-1 Vocational Student Visa

The M-1 visa is specifically designed for vocational and non-academic programs — including flight training. This is the standard visa for international students coming to the US specifically to earn their FAA pilot certificates.

  • ✓Issued for duration of program + 30 days
  • ✓Requires I-20 from PTC (Part 141)
  • ✓Full-time training required
  • ✓No work authorization (training only)
  • ✓Extendable through USCIS
  • ✓Dependents can apply for M-2 visa
Alternative Option

B-1/B-2 Visitor Visa

Visitors from certain countries may train in the US under a B-1/B-2 visa or visa waiver (ESTA) for limited recreational flight training — but this is not valid for full career-track programs like PPL → CPL. Always confirm eligibility with your immigration attorney.

  • ⚠Limited — recreational training only
  • ⚠Cannot pursue full career program
  • ⚠TSA FTSP clearance still required
  • →No I-20 required for B visa
  • →Max stay typically 6 months
Not recommended for students pursuing PPL, IFR, or CPL at PTC. Use M-1 visa instead.
Common Questions

International Student FAQ

Do I need to speak English fluently?

The FAA requires all pilots to be able to read, speak, write, and understand English. Radio communications are conducted in English. You don't need to be native-level fluent, but conversational English proficiency is essential for safe and effective training. PTC instructors are patient and supportive.

Can I bring my family with me?

Yes. Dependents (spouse and unmarried children under 21) can apply for an M-2 visa to accompany you. You will need to show proof of sufficient funds to support your family in addition to your training costs.

How long does the entire visa process take?

From submitting your application to PTC to arriving in the US typically takes 2–4 months. TSA/FTSP clearance takes 3–30 days. US Embassy visa processing varies by country — some appointments are available within weeks, others take months. Plan ahead and contact PTC early.

Will my FAA license be valid in my home country?

FAA certificates are internationally recognized and can often be converted to local licenses in many countries under ICAO agreements. Requirements vary by country — check with your home country's civil aviation authority (CAA). Many countries allow direct conversion of FAA Commercial licenses.

Can I work as a flight instructor in the US to build hours?

M-1 visa holders generally cannot work in the US. However, USCIS may allow practical training directly related to your program. Consult an immigration attorney regarding post-completion practical training options. Many international students return home to instruct and build hours after earning their CPL.

What if my visa is denied?

Visa denials are not uncommon, but most are resolvable. Common reasons include insufficient proof of funds, weak ties to home country, or incomplete documentation. PTC can help you strengthen your application with proper school documentation. You may reapply after addressing the denial reason.

Is La Verne a safe city for international students?

Yes — La Verne is consistently ranked as one of the safer cities in the greater Los Angeles area. It's a quiet suburban community with a strong university presence (University of La Verne) and a welcoming environment for international students and residents.

What airport do I fly into?

The closest international airports are Ontario International (ONT) — just 20 minutes from Brackett Airport — and Los Angeles International (LAX), about 50 minutes away. We recommend ONT for simplicity and cost.
