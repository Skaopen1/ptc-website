Enrollment Process

How to Get Started

From your first call to taking the controls — here's the complete domestic enrollment process.

01

Reach Out & Schedule a Tour

Contact PTC via phone, email, or the contact form on this site. We'll invite you to Brackett Airport to meet the team, walk the flight line, see the aircraft up close, and get all your questions answered. No commitment required — just come see what we're about.

Schedule a Tour
02

Book a Discovery Flight

Before committing to a program, experience what training actually feels like. During a discovery flight, you'll take the controls of a Cessna 172N over La Verne and the surrounding area with a certified flight instructor by your side. It's the best way to confirm this is the right path for you.

✈️ Hollywood Sign — $220
🏝️ Catalina Island — $300
03

Choose Your Program

Decide between Part 141 (structured, career-track, FAA-approved syllabus) or Part 61 (flexible, personalized, train on your schedule). Your instructor will help you pick based on your goals, availability, and how quickly you want to progress. Both paths lead to the same FAA certificates.

Compare Part 141 vs Part 61 →
04

Obtain Your FAA Medical Certificate

All student pilots must hold at least a 3rd Class FAA Medical Certificate before flying solo. You'll visit an Aviation Medical Examiner (AME) for a physical — typically takes under an hour and costs $75–$150. PTC can connect you with an AME near La Verne. Most students have no issues passing the 3rd Class exam.

💡 Tip: You can begin ground school and your written exam prep before your medical exam, so there's no reason to wait. Knock out both at the same time.
05

Enroll & Begin Flying

Complete your enrollment paperwork, get your FAA Student Pilot Certificate (free, issued online through IACRA), set your recurring schedule with your CFI, and take to the skies. Your aviation journey officially begins.

What You Need

Domestic Student Requirements

No prior experience needed. Here's what the FAA and PTC require before you can begin training.

🎂

Age Requirements

16 years minimum to fly solo. 17 years to earn your Private Pilot License. You can begin ground school and flight training at any age under a qualified CFI.

🏥

FAA Medical Certificate

3rd Class medical required for Private Pilot. 2nd Class for Commercial. Issued by an FAA Aviation Medical Examiner (AME). Most healthy individuals qualify easily.

🗣️

English Proficiency

FAA requires pilots to read, speak, write, and understand English. Radio communications at Brackett and throughout the LA airspace are conducted in English.

📋

Citizenship / Residency

US citizens and lawful permanent residents (green card holders) may train under Part 61 or Part 141 with no additional government screening beyond your FAA medical.

Part 141

Structured Career Training

FAA-approved syllabus, stage checks, defined milestones. Best for full-time students who want the fastest, most efficient path to certification.

  • FAA-approved structured syllabus
  • Reduced minimum flight hours
  • Stage checks and structured reviews
  • Best for career-track, full-time students
Learn More
Part 61

Flexible & Personalized

Train on your schedule. No fixed syllabus, no stage checks. Perfect for working professionals, students, and anyone who needs to train around life.

  • Flexible scheduling — fly when you can
  • Personalized to your pace and goals
  • No stage check requirements
  • Best for part-time, working adults
Learn More
